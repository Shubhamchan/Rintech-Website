GST REGISTRATION

01

GST Registration Online - An Overview

  • Goods and Services Tax (GST), introduced on 1st July 2017,applies to all service providers (including freelancers), traders and manufacturers in India.
  • Introduced on July 1, 2017, a number of central taxes such as service tax, excise tax, CST and state taxes such as entertainment tax, luxury tax, octroy and VAT have been combined into one tax (GST). rice field.
  • GST must be calculated at every stage of the supply chain. The GST process is completely online and requires no manual intervention.

02

WHAT ARE THE COMPONENT OF GST?

  • GST has three tax components:
  • A central component (Central Goods and Services Tax or CGST) and a state component (State Goods and Services Tax or SGST) where the central and state collect GST from all businesses.
  • Interstate transactions are accompanied by an Integrated Tax on Goods and Services (IGST) imposed by the Center.
  • When a transaction occurs from one state to another.

03

WHO NEEDS GST REGISTRATION SERVICES?

  • Any business or entity involved in the sale or purchase of services must register for GST online.
  • Businesses with an annual turnover exceeding his Rs. 2 lakh (providing services) and Rs. 4 lakh (supply of goods) are obliged to register for GST.
  • All businesses making cross-border deliveries of goods must also be registered for GST.
  • The same applies to companies making taxable supplies on behalf of other taxable persons. B. Agents and Brokers.
  • According to a recent notice, an E-commerce seller/aggregator is also not required to register if the total turnover is less than his Rs 20,000.
