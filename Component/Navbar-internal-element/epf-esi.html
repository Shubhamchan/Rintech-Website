WHAT IS EMPLOYEE PROVIDENT FUND?

  • The Employee Provident Fund is a scheme for Indian workers governed by the Provident Fund, 1952 and other provisions of the Act.
  • The Employee Provident Fund is regulated under the umbrella of the Employees Provident Fund Organization, commonly known as EPFO.
  • All establishments employing 20 or more employees are eligible to apply for PF registration in India.
  • In some cases, establishments with fewer than 20 employees are still eligible for her PF registration depending on the circumstances and exemptions.
  • Employees receive an amount that includes personal and employer contributions with interest when they retire or retire.

02

WHAT IS ESIC REGISTRATION?

  • The Employees National Insurance Corporation or ESIC is an Indian social insurance organization that provides cash and medical benefits to workers in the institutional sector of India.
  • An employer or company is required by law to register a company under her ESIC within 15 days of the effective date.
  • ESIC registration is the process of identifying and registering each employer and its employees in the system.
  • Any company with 10 or more employees and monthly income not exceeding Rs 15,000 is required to register with ESIC under the ESI Act, 1948.
  • As of today, all other states and Union Territories of India except Manipur, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh and Mizoram are required to comply with the ESI Act 1948.
  • As of March 31, 2013, approximately 6.66,000 employers are registered with ESIC, extending ESIC benefit protection to more than 1.65 million workers.
